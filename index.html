About

According to wikipedia, G20 is a comglomerate of 20 governments which was founded in 1999. The goal of the G20 is to develop policy with a goal of enhancing financial stability. G20 countries create the ninety percent of the gross world product and and eighty percent of world trade.

The members of the G20 are: Australia, Canada, Saudi Arabia, United States, India, Russia, South Africa, Turkey, Argentina, Brazil, Mexico, France, Germany, Italy, UK, China, Indonesia, Japan and South Korea.

This project aims to present, in a user-friendly way, financial information that relates to some of the countries mentioned above, with a focus on healthcare, military and education expenditures over various years.

In the charts, you will be able to observe the following information:

• Comparison of the spending data to that country’s GDP
• Comparison of the education and health to the overall military spending of the country
• Comparison of the per person educational spending to the per person GDP
• Comparison the per person healthcare spending to the per person GDP
• Comparison the per person military spending to the per person GDP
• Fastest growing countries in healthcare and educational spending in fixed value and in percentage
• Information was obtained from The World Bank

Spending Education Data vs Country’s GDP

The chart presented on the left side revels something suprinsing, the higher the GDP of a country is the less likely it is to spend on education.
For instance, if we compare The United States with Turkey, we can see that even though the US has a larger GDP (billions of USD), the education spending represents around 5 percent of the GDP.
On the other hand, Turkey with a relatively small GDP compare with The United States of America has an education spending around 12 percent.
That shows that Turkey between the years 2013 and 2018 invested much more in Education than the other countries in the G20 Group with more GDP.

Spending Healthcare Data vs Country’s GDP

This chart shows that most G20 countries spend similar amounts on the healthcare as a portion of GDP. curiously, those countries located in the European region fromm 2013 to 2018 have remained constant in their healthcare spending. Although the United States does not have a higher spending in Education, it has around 16 percent of spending in healthcare.

Particularly, China, India and most of the Asia region had had and spending in healtcare of 4 percentage in average during the five-year period mentioned before.

Spending Military Data vs Country’s GDP

This chart shows that most G20 countries spend similar amounts on the military as a portion of GDP. Interestingly, there are a few outliers. Notably, the United States devotes a much larger percentage of its GDP to the military than other G20 countries, while China spends a comparatively low amount on its military.

The data in this chart was relatively consistent in the years shown, indicating that the ratio of Military spending to GDP is fairly inelastic.

Eduaction Spending vs Military Spending

The chart shows that the education spending related to military spending. In most cases education expenditures have been higher than military expenditures with the exception of two countries: China and Japan.

China has been exponentially increasing its expenditures in military. On the other hand, we observe that at some point Japan increased its spending in education but later on this spending dropped. This same case can be observed for Turkey.

Healthcare Spending vs Military Spending

Although there are some increases in military expenditures given for China and the Unitated States, it is observed in the chart that the spending in military has been lower in comparison with healthcare.

We also can see some spikes in healthcare expenditures for countries such as China, France, Germany, Japan and the United States. Remarkable, The U.S. has been the biggest spender in healthcare and Argentina the lowest. According to the data from 2000 to 2018.

Education Spending vs GDP per Capita

In a period of 6 years from 2013 to 2018, the chart shows that the educations spending per capita is significative lower in comparison to the GDP per capita. Also, It can be observed that the expenditures in education per person have remained steadily during the 6 years.

Healthcare per capita vs GDP per Capita

It can be observed in the chart that the healthcare expenditures for some countries are higher than from others. For instance, the United States of America had the highest spending in healthcare in comparison with the other G20 countries.

On the other hand, Mexico, Russia, South Africa, Turkey and China had the lowest expenditures in healthcare. Surprising, there are other countries like India and Indonesia without any expenditure in healtcare for the year 2018. However, the India's and Indonesia's contries for the same period had GDP per capita relatively low in comparison to the other G20 countries.

Military per capita vs GDP per Capita

The chart shows that for the year 2018, some countries did not have any military expenditures.Among them are Argentina, Brazil, Indonesia and Turkey. Some of the other countries remain the same in the military spending. For instance, the United States of America and Saudi Arabia. Although, these two contries (the U.S and Saudi Arabia) are signficantlly different in regards to the GDP per capita, both had similar military expenditures.

Fastest growing countries in healthcare and educational spending in fixed value

The map showed on the left side belongs to the "Fastest growing countries in healthcare spending in fixed value", and the one located to the right side is "Fastest growing countries in educational spending in fixed values".

In both maps healtcare (left side) and education (right side), we can observe that the country with fastest growth during the period 2013 to 2018 was China, followed by India, Saudi Arabia, and The United States.

Fastest growing countries in healthcare and educational spending in percentage

In the chart we can observed the growth percentage among the nations that belong to the G20. we can see that during the 6-year period, the education growth in percentage was significative higher for China and Indonesia. Meanwhile, for Russia and Brazil the education growth was negative.

In contrast, Brasil had a postive healthcare growth.
